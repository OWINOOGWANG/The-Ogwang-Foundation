PATRONS

Elechi Amadi

He was an author of plays and novels that are generally about African village life, customs, beliefs, and religious practices prior to contact with the Western world.

Robert Johnson

BET founder Robert Johnson donated works from his private collection to the National Museum of African American History and Culture.He has been collecting fine art since the 1980s and, according to a news release, has assembled a private collection of more than 250 pieces of artwork by 19th and 20th century artists. Johnson acquired the Barnett-Aden Collection, which documents the struggles and achievements of black Americans, from the National Museum of African American Art in Florida in 1998.

Joseph Murumbi

He was probably the continent’s greatest private collector of art, books, postage stamps, artifacts, textiles, jewellery and everything African, including 50,000 documents on Africa, many of which he acquired during the decade that he was in exile in England before Kenyan Independence, a time when friendly British MPs (Members of Parliament) would save documents and information on Africa for him to collect and take away, which he gladly did.
